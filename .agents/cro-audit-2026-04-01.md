# CRO Audit — sprintzero.sh

*Date: 2026-04-01*

## Page Context
- **Page type:** Long-form sales page (single-page homepage)
- **Conversion goal:** Book a free audit call (SavvyCal)
- **Traffic context:** Early stage, mostly warm/network traffic, shifting to cold (LinkedIn, content, paid)
- **Current state:** 2 customers from network — page hasn't been pressure-tested with cold traffic yet

---

## Quick Wins (Implement Now)

### 1. Kill the modal. Link directly to SavvyCal.
The modal is pure friction. Every CTA opens a modal that restates what the page already says, then asks them to click another button. Two clicks for one thing. Move the "30 minutes. No pitch." framing inline near CTAs and link directly to the scheduler.

### 2. Add a logo bar.
3 customers (enCappture, Founderwell, SuccessionAPP). Add a small logo strip above the fold or after the marquee. Even 3 logos dramatically change the "is this real?" calculation for cold visitors.

### 3. Add UTM parameters to the SavvyCal link.
`https://savvycal.com/marcel-from-bold/sprint-zero` has zero tracking. Append `?utm_source=website&utm_medium=cta&utm_campaign=lp`.

### 4. Surface the scarcity signal.
"2 spots per month" is buried in modal footnotes. Put it in the hero subtext or near the hero CTA.

### 5. Add a sticky mobile CTA.
On mobile, the "Book Audit Call" button is hidden behind the hamburger menu. The next visible CTA after the hero requires scrolling through 7 dense sections. Add a sticky bottom bar on mobile.

---

## High-Impact Changes (Prioritize)

### 6. Add a CTA after the Case Study section.
Current: Hero → (7 sections, no CTA) → Pricing → (3 sections) → Bottom CTA. Massive dead zone. Put a CTA after the "200 hours → 6 hours" moment.

### 7. Move social proof higher.
Testimonials are section 8 of 14 — most visitors never reach them. Move at least one testimonial into or just below the hero.

### 8. Name the case study client (if possible).
Anonymous case study reads like fiction. Even a first name + company type helps.

### 9. Address the "vibe coding" objection above the fold.
Core buying anxiety only addressed in FAQ Q1 behind an accordion. Add explicit "this isn't vibe coding" messaging near the hero or process section.

### 10. Tighten the Pain section.
5 pain points with full paragraphs is too much. Options: cut to 3, shorten bodies, or accordion.

### 11. Comparison table needs a mobile redesign.
Most compelling sales argument requires horizontal scrolling on mobile. Switch to stacked card layout on mobile.

---

## Test Ideas

### 12. Hero variant: problem-first vs. provocation-first
Test a problem-first approach matching prospect's internal dialogue vs. current provocative style.

### 13. Test removing the marquee strip
Mixes proof, pricing, and claims in uncontrollable moving text. Test static logo bar + one proof stat instead.

### 14. Test a shorter page
14 sections is a lot. Test cutting Cost + Comparison into one section and removing ICP section.

### 15. Test CTA copy
- "Get Your Free Codebase Audit"
- "See What's Broken (Free, 30 min)"
- "Book a Free 30-Min Audit"
